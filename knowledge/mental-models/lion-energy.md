# Lion Energy

## In a Nutshell

Work in intense bursts, not steady marathons. Like a lion — long periods of conservation punctuated by explosive, focused effort. Role-rotate between Builder, Marketer, and Strategist to keep energy fresh. This reframes ADHD-style work patterns as an asset: hyperfocus windows are the bursts, and the "unproductive" recovery between them is what makes the bursts possible.

## How It Works

Three interlocking elements:

### 1. Burst Pattern

Deep work happens in concentrated 60-90 minute blocks, not 8-hour sustained sessions. The 5:30-7:00 AM maker block is the primary burst — highest energy, zero interruptions, most cognitively demanding work. The 7:30-8:30 PM block is the secondary burst — lower energy, better suited for lighter tasks like outreach and content.

The key insight: **the rest between bursts is not laziness.** It's recovery that enables the next burst. Eliminating the rest doesn't create more output — it destroys the burst capacity. A lion that hunts all day catches nothing.

### 2. Role Rotation

Solo founders wear multiple hats. Rather than fighting this, lean into it:
- **Builder** — code, design, product work (deep creative energy)
- **Marketer** — outreach, content, social, distribution (social/communicative energy)
- **Strategist** — planning, systems, brain repo, weekly review (analytical energy)

When energy for one role fades, rotate to another instead of forcing it. The AM burst is usually Builder energy. The PM burst shifts naturally to Marketer or Strategist energy. Trying to code at 8 PM after a full day job is fighting your biology — but writing a few social posts or reviewing the week's plan fits the energy level perfectly.

This echoes Michael Gerber's *E-Myth* framework (1986): every business owner is simultaneously a Technician (does the work), Manager (organizes the work), and Entrepreneur (envisions the work). The trap is spending all your time as the Technician because building feels productive. Role rotation forces you into Manager and Entrepreneur mode regularly.

### 3. Energy-Aware Scheduling

Match the task to the energy, not the calendar. If Builder energy shows up unexpectedly, ride it — even if the schedule says "marketing day." The schedule sets defaults; energy overrides them. This is the difference between a rigid system (which ADHD will rebel against) and a flexible one (which channels ADHD's strengths).

The weekly theme system supports this: assign a *default* theme per day (Monday = marketing, Tuesday = product), but allow swaps when energy doesn't match. The theme reduces decision fatigue; the swap permission prevents resentment.

## When to Use

- **Designing your daily schedule** — build around bursts, not sustained hours. Protect the burst windows ruthlessly.
- **When feeling guilty about not working 8 straight hours** — that model doesn't apply. 2.5 focused hours in bursts beats 6 scattered hours every time.
- **When choosing what task to do right now** — match the task to your current energy. Don't force Builder work when Marketer energy is what's available.
- **When burning out on one type of work** — rotate roles. The variety is the recovery mechanism.
- **When scheduling across the week** — use role rotation to ensure all three hats get worn, not just the comfortable one (Builder, usually).

## Example

The [Time Allocation](../systems/time-allocation.md) schedule is Lion Energy in practice. The AM block (5:30-7:00) is the lion's hunt — peak focus, hardest work, zero distractions. The day job is the long rest. The PM block (7:30-8:30) is a secondary, lighter hunt. The non-negotiables (family time, Marcie time, exercise) aren't interruptions — they're the ecosystem that sustains the bursts.

The "building is cheap so I only build" trap is the Technician hat from the E-Myth dominating the other roles. Lion Energy + role rotation breaks this: when Builder energy fades, switch to Marketer. When Marketer energy fades, switch to Strategist. The portfolio needs all three.

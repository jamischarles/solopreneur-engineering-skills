# Now/Next/Later

## In a Nutshell

Organize work into three confidence horizons — Now (committed, in progress), Next (shaped, ready when a slot opens), Later (ideas, not yet shaped). No dates. No false precision. Confidence decreases as you move right. This replaces traditional roadmaps that pretend you know what you'll ship in Q4.

## Source

Janna Bastow / ProdPad — originally a product management framework for honest roadmaps. Widely adopted because it solves the same problem at every scale: how do you communicate priority without lying about timelines?

## How It Works

Three columns. That's the whole framework.

**Now** — High confidence. Actively being worked on. WIP-limited (if everything is "Now," nothing is). These are real commitments with shaped scope and clear next actions. In a solo context, Now = the current week's bet.

**Next** — Medium confidence. Shaped enough to start soon. Ordered by priority, but no start dates. When a Now slot opens, the top Next item gets pulled in. These have been thought through — you know *what* you'd build, just not *when*.

**Later** — Low confidence. Unvalidated ideas, vague intentions, "wouldn't it be cool if..." items. Unordered. No due dates. No commitments. Later is a parking lot, not a promise. Items here cost nothing to hold and nothing to drop.

The key discipline: **Later items have no dates and no commitments.** They are options, not obligations. The moment you put a date on a Later item, you've created a fake plan that will either disappoint someone or distort your priorities.

Items flow left as they earn confidence. A Later idea gets shaped → moves to Next. A Next item gets prioritized → moves to Now. Movement is earned, not scheduled.

## When to Use

- **Product roadmaps** — when someone asks "when will X ship?" Answer with the horizon (Now/Next/Later), not a date.
- **Weekly planning** — the Sunday review sorts work into these three buckets for the coming week.
- **Property prioritization** — which properties get maker time this week (Now), which are queued (Next), which are parked (Later)?
- **When fighting the urge to commit to everything** — Later is the pressure release valve. It says "yes, this matters, but not yet" without creating a fake deadline.
- **When an existing plan feels dishonest** — if your roadmap has dates you don't believe, replace dates with horizons.

## Example

The properties portfolio is a Now/Next/Later board in practice:

- **Now:** Properties at Tier 2-3 with active development — the current sprint focus.
- **Next:** Tier 1 properties with active experiments — shaped enough to pick up when a slot opens (e.g., Pierre Notabot with content ready to publish).
- **Later:** Parked properties — Respite, MonoCal, Bifrost. No timeline. No guilt. They'll move to Next if signal appears.

Same pattern at the task level within a single property: the current bet's scope is Now, the next bet's pitch is Next, the brainstorm list is Later.

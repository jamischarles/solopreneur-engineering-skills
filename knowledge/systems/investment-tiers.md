# Investment Tiers

## Purpose

A system for deciding how much time, energy, and craft to invest in any initiative — product, feature, content piece, or marketing channel. The default is *less*, not more. Things earn investment by proving they deserve it.

This is the connective tissue between [Tiny Bets](../mental-models/tiny-bets.md) (place many small bets) and [Minimum Effective Dose](../principles/minimum-effective-dose.md) (keep each bet small). The tiers answer: "This thing exists — now how much do I feed it?"

## Philosophy

With 2.5 hours a day, over-investing is more dangerous than under-investing. A mediocre bet that gets three months of polish is three months of other bets you never placed. The system is designed to prevent that.

**Everything starts at Tier 1.** No exceptions. You earn your way up with signal, not with enthusiasm.

Think of it like poker: most hands, you fold. Some hands, you call. Very rarely, you go all-in. The discipline is in the folding, not the betting.

## The Three Tiers

### Tier 1 — Explore

**Investment:** Minimum Effective Dose only. Weekend builds. Quick experiments. Ship and measure.

**Signal required to enter:** None. Gut feel is enough. The whole point is that bets are cheap enough to place without justification.

**What this looks like:**
- Build the core feature, skip the settings screen
- Ship to TestFlight, not the App Store
- Tweet the idea, don't write the blog post
- One landing page, not a full marketing site
- No branding beyond a clean name

**Time budget:** A few hours to a few days per initiative. If it's taking longer, you're over-investing for this tier.

**Exit criteria:**
- **Promote to Tier 2** when you see real signal: users returning, people sharing, revenue, inbound DMs, organic growth
- **Kill or shelve** when there's silence after a fair exposure window. No guilt — most bets live and die here.

### Tier 2 — Double Down

**Investment:** Sustained effort. Iteration. Active marketing. This thing has earned your attention.

**Signal required to enter:** Demonstrated traction. Not "I think this could work" — actual evidence that people want this. Examples:
- Users are coming back without being prompted
- People are sharing it with others
- You're getting unsolicited feedback or feature requests
- Early revenue or signup velocity
- Content about it gets above-average engagement

**What this looks like:**
- Regular development cycles — bug fixes, feature additions, polish
- Active marketing: social posts, outreach, content about the product
- Proper onboarding and user experience improvements
- Analytics and tracking to measure growth
- Newsletter mentions, build writeups, demo content

**Time budget:** Dedicated blocks in your weekly schedule. This is now a "real" project, not an experiment.

**Exit criteria:**
- **Promote to Tier 3** when there's sustained demand, personal conviction, and the potential to become a signature product — something worth being known for
- **Demote to Tier 1** if signal fades. Traction can be a blip. If growth stalls and you've given it a real push, move it back down. No sunk cost reasoning.

### Tier 3 — Craft

**Investment:** Real TLC. This is where you go Ugmonk. Hours on branding. Midjourney assets. Polished marketing copy. Twitter bio mentions. The works.

**Signal required to enter:** All of the following:
- Sustained demand over weeks/months (not a one-time spike)
- Personal conviction — you believe in this and want to be known for it
- Clear path to being a signature product or content pillar
- You're willing to make this one of your top 2 focus areas

**What this looks like:**
- Crafted brand identity — custom visuals, consistent aesthetic, intentional positioning
- Marketing that you'd be proud to show anyone — polished landing pages, compelling copy
- Deep feature work — not just "good enough" but "this is clearly made by someone who cares"
- App Store optimization, proper screenshots, video previews
- The kind of care where someone picks it up and *feels* the quality (the Ugmonk test)

**Time budget:** Significant portion of maker time. This is where sprint modes (Ship Sprint, Launch Sprint) get activated.

**Hard rule: maximum 2 things at Tier 3 at any time.** With 2.5 hours a day, you cannot craft three things well. Pick two. Everything else stays at Tier 1 or 2.

```
        ▼
   ┌─────────┐

   │ Explore │

        │
   Real signal? ──── No ──→ Shelve or kill. No guilt.
        │
       Yes
        ▼
   ┌──────────┐
   │ Tier 2   │  Iterate. Market. Grow.
   │ Double   │
   │ Down     │
   └────┬─────┘
        │
   Sustained demand + ──── No ──→ Stay at Tier 2
   conviction + capacity?          (or demote to Tier 1 if signal fades)
        │
       Yes

   │ Tier 3   │  Ugmonk-level craft. Signature product.

```

## Rules

1. **Everything starts at Tier 1.** No skipping. Excitement is not signal.
2. **Max 2 at Tier 3.** Non-negotiable. If you want to promote something, demote something else first.
3. **Demotions are healthy.** Moving something down isn't failure — it's the system working. You learned what doesn't have pull.
4. **Review tiers weekly.** Part of the Sunday weekly review. Takes 5 minutes. "Is anything showing signal I should promote? Is anything stalled that I should demote?"
5. **Tier determines time allocation.** Don't spend Tier 3 time on Tier 1 projects. If you catch yourself polishing a Tier 1 project for hours, stop.

## Integration with Other Systems

- **[Time Allocation](time-allocation.md):** Tier assignments inform how maker time is split across properties. Tier 2-3 properties get dedicated blocks. Tier 1 properties get leftover time or dedicated experiment slots.
- **[Product Pipeline](product-pipeline.md):** Tiers are orthogonal to pipeline phases. A Tier 1 product can be in any phase — the tier governs *how much* you invest, the pipeline tracks *where* it is in the lifecycle.
- **[Sprint Modalities](time-allocation.md):** Sprints are a Tier 2-3 tool. Don't activate a Ship Sprint for a Tier 1 experiment.

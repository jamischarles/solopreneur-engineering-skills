# Daniel Priestley — 7-11-4 Content & Authority Playbook

## In a Nutshell

People buy from people they feel they know, like, and trust. To reach that threshold, a prospect needs 7 hours of your content, across 11 touchpoints, in 4 different locations. Build the content ecosystem that makes this happen on autopilot.

## Source

Daniel Priestley — *Key Person of Influence*, *Oversubscribed*, *24 Assets*, *Scorecard Marketing*, and his social media content.

## The Framework

### Step 1: Become a Key Person of Influence (The 5 P's)

Work through these five areas in order — each feeds the next:

1. **Pitch** — Articulate your value clearly. If you can't explain what you do in 60 seconds, nothing else matters.
2. **Publish** — Write a book, blog, podcast, or create IP. Publishing is the ultimate credibility signal.
3. **Product** — Turn your expertise into something scalable (course, software, productized service) so you're not trading time for money.
4. **Profile** — Build a visible, recognizable presence in your niche. Be the name people think of first.
5. **Partnership** — Form strategic alliances that expand reach. Partnerships generate insights that improve your pitch — closing the loop.

### Step 2: Build 7 Hours of Content

Create enough content that a prospect can consume 7 hours of you before they ever speak to you. This creates a parasocial relationship — they feel like they *know* you.

Types of content that stack toward 7 hours:
- Long-form YouTube videos or podcast episodes
- Blog posts and articles
- An introduction presentation (45–90 min, covers what you do and why)
- A book (the single highest-trust content asset)
- Email sequences and newsletters
- Social media posts and short-form video

### Step 3: Create 11 Touchpoints Across 4 Locations

Spread your content across at least 4 platforms/channels so prospects encounter you in different contexts:

| Location | Example Touchpoints |
|---|---|
| Social media | Posts, reels, stories, comments |
| Long-form content | YouTube, podcast, blog, newsletter |
| Direct outreach | Email sequences, DMs, webinars |
| In-person / live | Speaking events, workshops, live streams |

Each encounter is a touchpoint. You need 11 before someone feels ready to buy.

### Step 4: Become Oversubscribed

Once the content engine is running, engineer demand to exceed supply:

1. **Know your capacity** — how many customers can you actually serve?
2. **Signal and build up** — use content + touchpoints to generate interest beyond capacity
3. **Release when oversubscribed** — only open the offer when demand outstrips supply
4. **Deliver remarkably** — set expectations slightly lower than what you deliver
5. **Celebrate and innovate** — share wins publicly, then evolve the offering

### Step 5: Repurpose Everything

One substantial piece of content becomes many:
- A 60-minute video → 10 short clips → 20 social posts → 5 newsletter issues → 1 blog post
- A book → a 12-month program → a scorecard → speaking content → social media quotes
- One idea, 100+ distributed assets

## Key Concepts

- **7-11-4 Rule** — 7 hours of content, 11 touchpoints, 4 locations. The threshold for trust.
- **Warm vs. cold audience** — warm audiences need less context and convert faster. The content engine warms people up before you ever pitch them.
- **Scorecard marketing** — use assessments/quizzes to educate prospects while collecting data. Great for lead gen that doesn't feel like lead gen.
- **24 Assets** — a business should have 24 assets that generate value without your direct involvement (IP, content, brand, systems, products, partnerships, etc.).
- **Attraction over promotion** — create enough value that opportunities come to you rather than being chased.
- **Micro-niche authority** — don't try to be known by everyone. Be the most well-known, respected, and sought-after person in a specific niche.

## How I Apply It

- Build toward 7 hours of content across my properties (newsletter, social, YouTube, blog)
- Use the CAPSTONE pitch structure to tighten how I introduce what I do
- Create a scorecard/assessment as a lead gen tool
- Repurpose every long-form piece into at least 5 shorter assets
- Focus on becoming the Key Person of Influence in my specific niche

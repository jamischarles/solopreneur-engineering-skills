# Personal Kanban

## In a Nutshell

Make your work visible and limit how much you do at once. Two rules: (1) visualize your work on a board, (2) limit your work-in-progress. That's it. The magic is in the WIP limit — it forces you to finish before starting, which is the single hardest discipline for a solo builder with too many ideas.

## Source

Jim Benson & Tonianne DeMaria Barry — *Personal Kanban: Mapping Work, Navigating Life* (2011). Adapted from Toyota's production system (Taiichi Ohno) and David Anderson's *Kanban* (2010) for knowledge work. Personal Kanban strips it down to the essentials for individuals and tiny teams.

## The Framework

### 1. Visualize your work

Put every task on a board with at minimum three columns: **Ready**, **Doing**, **Done**. Physical sticky notes, a digital board, or even a text file — the medium doesn't matter. What matters is that you can see everything at once. Hidden work is unmanaged work.

### 2. Limit work-in-progress (WIP)

Set a hard cap on how many items can be in the Doing column. For a solo builder with 2.5 hours/day, a WIP limit of 2-3 is realistic. When the Doing column is full, nothing new enters until something moves to Done. This is the core discipline — it fights the "start everything, finish nothing" pattern.

### 3. Pull, don't push

When a Doing slot opens, *pull* the highest-priority item from Ready. Don't let external pressure push new work into Doing beyond the WIP limit. The board is the contract.

### 4. Celebrate Done

The Done column exists for a reason — it makes progress visible. Regularly review it. It's proof that you're shipping, not just spinning.

## Key Concepts

- **WIP limits are the whole game** — without them, a Kanban board is just a to-do list with columns. The limit is what creates flow and forces finishing.
- **Stop starting, start finishing** — the mantra. Every new thing you start dilutes attention on everything already in progress.
- **Pull-based flow** — work moves through the system at the pace of completion, not at the pace of new ideas arriving.
- **Visual = cognitive offload** — your brain stops holding the mental inventory. The board holds it. This is especially valuable with ADHD.
- **Blockers become visible** — if something sits in Doing for days, the board exposes it. You can't hide a stuck task in a long list.

## How I Apply It

- **The two-project-max rule IS a WIP limit.** The [Time Allocation](../systems/time-allocation.md) system caps context switches at 2 per day. That's Kanban theory in practice — with 2.5 hours, switching more than twice guarantees nothing ships.
- **Board for the week:** Ready = tasks shaped during Sunday review. Doing = the 2-3 things I'm actively working on. Done = what shipped this week.
- **Complement to Shape Up:** A [shaped bet](shape-up.md) sets the appetite (time budget). Kanban governs the flow of tasks *within* that bet — don't start 5 tasks inside a bet; pull 2, finish them, pull 2 more.
- **Milou as the board.** The role menu and task list function as a digital Kanban board. WIP limits are enforced by limiting menu choices to 2-4 per role.

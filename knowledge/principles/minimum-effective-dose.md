# Minimum Effective Dose

## In a Nutshell

The smallest input that produces the desired output. Anything beyond that is waste — and when you have 2.5 hours a day, waste is fatal.

## Source

Tim Ferriss — *The 4-Hour Body*. Borrowed from pharmacology: the minimum effective dose (MED) is the smallest dose that produces the desired outcome. Water boils at 100°C — higher temperatures don't make it "more boiled," they just waste energy.

Ferriss applied this to fitness, learning, and business. The insight transfers directly to building products and content with extreme time constraints.

## Why It Works

Two reasons:

1. **It protects your scarcest resource.** When time is genuinely limited, the difference between MED and "a little extra polish" is the difference between shipping three things this week and shipping one. Over-investing in one bet starves all the others.

2. **It forces you to find the leverage point.** When you ask "what's the minimum that would work?", you're forced to identify the one thing that actually matters. The MVP feature. The core message. The single distribution channel. Everything else is noise you're adding because it feels productive.

The trap: "minimum" feels like cutting corners. It's not. It's cutting *waste*. The MED for a tweet is a clear thought in 280 characters — not a rushed, sloppy tweet. The MED for a landing page is a clear value prop and a signup form — not a half-finished page. Quality at the right scope, not low quality at every scope.

## How I Apply It

- **Product:** What's the smallest version that's useful? Ship that. Not the version with settings screens and onboarding flows — the version that solves the core problem.
- **Content:** What's the smallest format that carries this idea? A tweet, not a blog post. A blog post, not a video series. Expand only when signal demands it (see Content Expansion Ladder).
- **Marketing:** What's the one channel that reaches my audience? Go deep there. Don't spread across five platforms at minimum effort each — that's not MED, that's minimum viable everything (which is maximum waste).
- **Process:** What's the smallest system that keeps me on track? A daily log and a weekly review. Not a dashboard with twelve metrics.
- **Branding:** MED for most properties is a clean name and a solid product. Ugmonk-level brand craft is reserved for the few things that have earned it (see Investment Tiers).

The daily question: "Is this the minimum that would work, or am I gold-plating?"

# Life Mission

> The family mission. What we're building toward — and what we're not.
> Last updated: 2026-04-04

---

## The Short Version

We have enough. Any excess is bonus — and bonus gets invested in helping other people improve their lives. That's it. That's the mission.

## Why This Exists

The [manifesto](../brand/jamischarles/manifesto.md) captures the fisherman parable — protect the life you already have, don't sacrifice it chasing more. This document goes one layer deeper: what do we actually do once we have enough? The answer Marcie and I keep coming back to is simple. We help people.

Not in the "write a check and feel good" way. In the "help people help themselves" way. Uplevel skills. Teach financial responsibility. Improve quality of life. The goal is to make people more capable and self-sufficient — not more dependent.

## What "Enough" Looks Like — Practically

We're a family of seven. "Enough" isn't abstract for us. It looks like this:

- **Family vacations:** Adjoining hotel rooms at a 3-4 star hotel — not extra beds crammed into one room for seven people. Comfortable, not lavish.
- **Adult trips:** Every few years, Marcie and I take a nice international trip. Not every year. Not budget. A real trip.
- **Business operating budget:** Enough to run the business, cover tools, hosting, and experiments without stress.
- **Play money:** A little for the few nice things we actually care about. Not a lifestyle. A handful of things.
- **The rest goes to the foundation.**

We don't want to be wealthy to live the wealthy lifestyle. We want to be wealthy enough to help people in meaningful ways — and comfortable enough that our family isn't sacrificing to do it.

## Division of Roles

Marcie and I have different superpowers, and we're leaning into them instead of fighting them.

### Jamis — The Builder

Building things, sharing technical knowledge, creating products, writing — all of this feels like play to me. It's almost effortless. I will gladly own everything on the maker side:

- Business operations and product development
- Content creation, teaching, technical writing
- All the things that generate the income

I keep a reasonable operating budget and a percentage of gains for myself. As gains grow, that percentage shrinks. The excess flows to the foundation.

### Marcie — The Steward

Managing money, financial planning, investments, investment properties, growing capital — all of this feels like play to Marcie. She owns all of it:

- The foundation and all excess money generated by the business and side projects
- Investment strategy — how to grow the money responsibly and ethically
- Supporting our children financially in responsible ways
- Systems like shared mortgage accounts (we become the no-interest lender for our kids — they pay us back, building discipline without paying a bank)

This isn't delegation. It's alignment. We each take the pieces that feel like play and hand off the pieces that don't.

## The Foundation (Mental Model)

This isn't a legal entity — not yet. It's a mental model for how excess money flows once we have enough.

**How it works:**
- All excess beyond "enough" flows into the foundation
- Marcie manages it — investments, allocation, strategy
- Money is invested responsibly and ethically
- The foundation funds efforts that help people improve their lives

**What the foundation is about:**
- Education (the specifics are still undefined — this is a future area to shape)
- Helping people uplevel their skills
- Teaching financial responsibility
- Improving quality of life for people who are willing to help themselves

**What the foundation is NOT about:**
- Financial handouts
- Creating dependency
- Making people reliant on ongoing support
- Charity theater

The goal is always: help people help themselves.

## Core Principles

1. **We have enough.** This is the starting point, not something we're working toward.
2. **Excess is bonus.** Any money beyond enough gets invested responsibly and ethically — not spent on lifestyle inflation.
3. **Maximize impact after enough.** Once we have what we need, the question becomes: how many people can we help?
4. **Marcie manages the money.** All of it. The foundation, the investments, the financial strategy. It's her domain.
5. **Building is play.** Sharing technical knowledge, creating products, shipping things — Jamis leans all the way into this. It's effortless. It's the engine.

## Education Vision

_This section is intentionally sparse. Education will likely be a major pillar of how we help people — but the specifics are still taking shape. We'll fill this in as it becomes clearer._

The broad direction: help people uplevel their skills and improve their quality of life. Not just technical skills — though that's where Jamis has the most to offer. The foundation may fund or support educational efforts that go well beyond tech.

## Voice & Tone

### The Energy

Zero following energy. I post what I want to talk about. If you're here, great — let's have a sharp conversation. If not, I'm building anyway.

Think: MKBHD's quiet confidence meets Pieter Levels' radical transparency meets Tobias van Schneider's craft obsession.

### What I Sound Like

- Opinionated but not preachy
- Direct but not aggressive
- Curious but not scattered
- Technical but accessible
- Classy, not edgy
- Dry humor, not performative

### What I Don't Sound Like

- "Hey guys! So excited to share..."
- Engagement-bait questions
- Humble-bragging about hustle
- Complaining about the industry
- Hot takes for the sake of hot takes
- "Building in public" as an identity (I just build; the public part is a byproduct)

### Jimmy Carr Principle

My online persona is a curated slice of who I am — not the whole picture. The public version is confident, opinionated, and craft-focused. The family version (dad of five, husband, faith life) exists but is not performed online. Responsive disclosure: share depth when it earns trust and context, not as a hook.

## Content Philosophy

### Principles

1. **Craft over content.** The builds come first. Content is the sawdust from making things, not a product in itself.
2. **Signal over noise.** Every post should pass the test: "Would I want to read this from someone else?"
3. **Show the work, not the wins.** Dead ends are as valuable as launches. Process over polish.
4. **Opinions are free. Positions cost something.** Take stands. Back them up. Be willing to be wrong publicly.
5. **No content treadmill.** I ship when I have something worth shipping. Consistency comes from always building, not from a posting schedule.

### What I Will Talk About

- Things I'm building and why
- Technical craft: architecture decisions, tradeoffs, interesting problems
- AI-assisted development: honest takes on what works and what doesn't
- The experience of making things as a person with a full life (5 kids, day job, real constraints)
- Industry opinions where I have a genuine position
- Historical patterns and shifts in technology
- Interactive, visual explorations of ideas

### What I Won't Talk About

- My family in detail (they're mentioned as context, never as content)
- My employer by name in any critical context
- Political hot takes
- Relationship advice / personal development content
- Anything I don't have firsthand experience with
- Anything that feels like performing rather than sharing

## Visual Identity (Minimal Viable Brand)

### Current Constraints (Not a Brand Book)

- **Typography:** One clean sans-serif everywhere (Inter, Geist, or DM Sans — pick one, commit)
- **Palette:** Mostly monochrome. One accent color for links/highlights. Clean, not flashy.
- **Photography:** One good headshot, consistent across all platforms
- **Site aesthetic:** Minimal, fast, content-forward. No hero animations. No gradients. Let the work speak.
- **Code/project screenshots:** Consistent terminal theme and editor appearance when showing work

### What Evolves Later

- Interactive essay visual signature (will emerge from doing)
- Recurring visual formats for Weekend Build, Dead End Builds, etc.
- Any logo or wordmark (unnecessary until proven otherwise)

### Jack Butcher Lesson

The visual constraints exist to remove decision fatigue and enable consistency — not to create a "brand." The identity is the voice and the work. The visuals just need to not get in the way.

## Key Decisions Made

1. **Real name, not a pseudonym.** The tone I want (opinionated, authoritative, zero-following-energy) only works attached to a person. Individual properties (apps, tools) can have their own names.
2. **jamischarles.com as the hub.** Everything points here. Social is distribution, not the destination.
3. **No content calendar.** Build things. Write about building things. Post when it's ready.
4. **Visual identity = constraints, not a brand book.** Pick font, pick palette, ship. Let the signature emerge from the work.
5. **LinkedIn is a channel, not a cringe factory.** Builder posts about interesting learnings. No "I'm humbled to announce..." energy.

## The Anxiety Acknowledgment

I have historically not done this because of anxiety, ADD, and competing commitments. What's different now:

- **AI tooling genuinely compresses the build-ship-write cycle.** This is not cope — it's a real structural change.
- **The "zero following energy" posture removes the pressure to perform.** I'm not committing to an audience. I'm committing to building in public.
- **The format (Weekend Builds, Dead End Builds) is time-boxed.** It doesn't become an open-ended obligation.
- **The priority stack hasn't changed:** Marcie → Kids → Work → Calling → Side Income. This fits under "Side Income" and doesn't move up.

If this starts feeling like a burden rather than an extension of what I already do, I stop. No guilt. The builds continue regardless.

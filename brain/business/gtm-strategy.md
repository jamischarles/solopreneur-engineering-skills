# Go-to-Market Strategy

> Created April 2026. Derives from the [manifesto](../brand/jamischarles/manifesto.md) — build what you want, ship, see what resonates. This document adds the missing piece: a lightweight system for actually getting products in front of real people.

## Core Approach: Personal-Brand-Led GTM

At this stage, nobody knows "Save My Brain Co" or "Uplevel Co." They know Jamis Charles. All GTM flows through the personal brand:

> "I'm Jamis, I build things. Here's a thing I built. Try it."

**What this means:**
- One Twitter (@jamischarles), not separate company accounts
- One blog/newsletter, not company-specific ones
- Each product gets its own landing page, but "about the maker" always links back to Jamis
- Reddit posts come from you as a person, not a company
- Company brands are internal organization only — they become real brands later, if/when a product takes off on its own

**When to spin out a company brand:**
- A product hits 1,000+ users or generates consistent revenue
- The audience for that product doesn't overlap with your personal brand audience
- Until then, keep it simple: one face, one voice, many products

## The Launch Sprint System

A 2-week focused cycle for taking a single property from "built" to "in front of real people."

### How It Works

1. **Pick one property** for a 2-week sprint
2. **Week 1 — Polish & Prep:** Fix top 3 embarrassing bugs, build landing page, record demo
3. **Week 2 — Ship & Measure:** Post publicly (Reddit, Twitter, communities), track response
4. **After the sprint:** Score the signal, move to the next property
5. **After 3-4 sprints:** Review all scores — double down on the winner

### Sprint Playbook (Step by Step)

#### Step 1: Polish (2-3 days)
- Fix the top 3 bugs/rough edges that would embarrass you
- NOT perfection — the bar is "would I be okay if a stranger saw this?"
- Write a one-paragraph description: what it does and who it's for

#### Step 2: Landing Page (1-2 days)
- One page. Must have: what it does, screenshot/demo, how to get it
- No "features" section with 12 bullets. Just: problem → solution → try it
- This feels like building (good — lean into it)

#### Step 3: Demo Video (1 day)
- 60-90 seconds. Screen recording + your voice
- Show the product doing the ONE thing it does best
- Post natively on Twitter + embed on landing page
- Tools: QuickTime screen record → basic trim → done. Real, not polished.

#### Step 4: Cold Posts (2-3 days)
- **Reddit:** Find 2-3 subreddits where your target user hangs out. Write a genuine post: "I built X because I had Y problem. Here's what it does. Would love feedback."
  - NOT "check out my app!" — that gets deleted
  - Share the story, show the demo, ask for feedback
- **Twitter:** Post the demo video with context: what you built, why, link
- **Hacker News:** "Show HN: [Product] — [one-line description]" (save for your strongest product)

#### Step 5: Measure (ongoing)
- How many people visited the landing page?
- How many signed up / downloaded / used it?
- Did anyone comment, DM, or share?
- What questions did people ask? (these become your FAQ / marketing copy)

### Signal Scoring

After each sprint, score 0-3 on each dimension:

| Dimension | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
|-----------|---|---|---|---|
| **Usage** | No one tried it | A few clicked, none stayed | Some signed up / downloaded | People are actually using it |
| **Sharing** | Zero | Someone mentioned it | Someone shared unprompted | Multiple people shared |
| **Stranger engagement** | Crickets | Polite comments | Real questions / feedback | People requesting features |
| **Your energy** | Felt like a chore | Neutral | Enjoyed parts of it | Couldn't stop thinking about it |

**8+ out of 12 → give it a second sprint. Under 4 → park it.**

## The 30-Minute GTM Tax

Instead of splitting time 50/50 (which turns marketing into a second job), do this:

**First 30 minutes of every maker session → one GTM action. Then build freely for 2 hours.**

One action means ONE of these:
- Write one Reddit post about what you're building
- Record one 2-minute demo video
- Build one section of a landing page
- Reply to comments/DMs from users
- Check analytics and write down what you learned
- Write one "build in public" tweet with a screenshot

**Why first:** If GTM goes last, it gets skipped. Building is play — it'll happen regardless. GTM needs the protected slot.

**The streak rule:** Track whether you did your 30-min GTM action each day. If you skip 3 days in a row, something is wrong — reassess the current sprint.

## Revenue Phasing

Aligned with the [investment tiers](../knowledge/systems/investment-tiers.md) philosophy:

1. **Sprints 1-3 (months 1-2):** Everything free. Goal is USERS, not revenue.
2. **Sprint 4+ (month 3):** For the highest-traction product, add a Pro tier or paid feature.
3. **Revenue targets:**
   - Months 1-2: $0 (learning what resonates)
   - Month 4: $100/month
   - Month 6: $500/month

Don't charge before you have users who love the free version. Premature monetization kills momentum.

## Unified vs. Separate (Phased)

**Phase 1 (now → 6 months): Unified through personal brand**
- One Twitter, one blog, one face behind everything
- Cross-pollination is fine: "I build attention tools AND AI education tools"
- Each product has its own landing page but the maker story connects them

**Phase 2 (6+ months, only if a product takes off): Spin out the winner**
- If Ration gets 1,000+ users → Save My Brain Co becomes a real brand with its own presence
- If Pierre Notabot gets real traffic → Uplevel Co becomes a real brand
- Until then, company brands are internal organization only

## Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Sprint model becomes permission to never focus | Hard rule: no new properties until all current ones get a sprint |
| "Not ready" feeling kills every sprint before launch | Define "good enough" on day 1, write it down, ship when you hit it |
| GTM 30-min tax gets skipped | Track as a streak. 3 skips in a row = reassess |
| Zero signal from all sprints | After 4 sprints with no traction → problem is product-market fit, not GTM. Do user interviews. |
| Time-sensitive windows missed | Start Sprint 1 THIS WEEK. Planning is done. |

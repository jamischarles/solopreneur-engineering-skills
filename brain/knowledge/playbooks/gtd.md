# GTD (Getting Things Done)

## In a Nutshell

Get everything out of your head and into a trusted system, then systematically decide what to do about each thing. Your brain is for having ideas, not holding them. The weekly review is the engine that keeps the whole system trustworthy — skip it and the system decays within days.

## Source

David Allen — *Getting Things Done: The Art of Stress-Free Productivity* (2001, revised 2015). The most widely adopted personal productivity methodology. Core thesis: your mind is a terrible office — it tracks open loops obsessively and unreliably. Offload everything to an external system so your mind can focus on execution.

## The Framework

Five stages, in order:

### 1. Capture

Get every open loop out of your head into an inbox — tasks, ideas, commitments, worries, "I should..." thoughts. Use whatever captures fastest: voice memo, text message, notebook, Telegram bot. The key is *ubiquitous capture* — never trust yourself to remember. If it crosses your mind, it goes in the inbox.

### 2. Clarify

Process each item in the inbox. For each one, ask:
- **Is it actionable?** If no → trash it, file it as reference, or put it on Someday/Maybe.
- **If yes → what's the next action?** Not "work on the website" but "draft the hero section copy." The next action is the specific, physical thing you'd do next.
- **Will it take less than 2 minutes?** If yes → do it now. The overhead of tracking it exceeds the cost of doing it.
- **Am I the right person?** If no → delegate it (Waiting For list).
- **Is it a multi-step project?** If yes → define the project and identify its next action.

### 3. Organize

Put clarified items where they belong:
- **Next Actions** — organized by context (@deep-work, @light-work, @errands, @phone)
- **Projects** — any outcome requiring more than one action step
- **Waiting For** — things delegated or dependent on someone else
- **Someday/Maybe** — not committed, but you don't want to lose the idea
- **Calendar** — only hard-landscape items (meetings, deadlines, time-specific)
- **Reference** — information you might need later, not actionable

### 4. Reflect (Weekly Review)

The weekly review is non-negotiable — it's what makes the system trustworthy. Every week:
- Clear all inboxes to zero
- Review all active projects — is each one moving? What's the next action?
- Review Waiting For — follow up on anything stale
- Review Someday/Maybe — promote or delete
- Review the calendar — what's coming? What needs prep?
- Get current, get clear, get creative

### 5. Engage

Do the work. Choose what to do based on four criteria:
1. **Context** — what can I do right now given where I am and what tools I have?
2. **Time available** — how much time do I have before the next commitment?
3. **Energy** — what's my current energy level? Match the task to it.
4. **Priority** — of the remaining options, what's the highest impact?

## Key Concepts

- **Ubiquitous capture** — brain is for *having* ideas, not *holding* them. If you trust your system, your mind relaxes.
- **Next action** — the single most impactful concept. Vague tasks ("fix the website") create resistance. Concrete next actions ("open Figma and sketch the new nav layout") create momentum.
- **Two-minute rule** — if it takes less than 2 minutes, do it immediately. Don't add overhead for trivial tasks.
- **Weekly review = the engine** — without it, lists go stale, trust erodes, and you're back to keeping everything in your head.
- **Someday/Maybe = pressure release valve** — ideas that aren't commitments go here. It prevents guilt about not doing everything while ensuring nothing is lost.
- **Contexts** — group actions by where/how you do them, not by project. This means when you sit down for deep work, you see ALL deep-work tasks across all projects.

## How I Apply It

- **Weekly review = Sunday review** in the [Time Allocation](../systems/time-allocation.md) system. 15 minutes. Review actual vs. target allocation, plan next week.
- **Capture = Milou's Telegram flow.** `/feature`, `/frm`, `/journal` are GTD capture tools. Ideas go to the inbox without friction.
- **The trusted system is brain + Milou.** The brain repo holds reference material and systems. Milou holds actionable items and projects. Together they're the external mind.
- **Contexts map to energy/time blocks.** The AM deep work block (5:30-7:00) is @deep-work context. The PM block (7:30-8:30) is @light-work. Match next actions to the current block.
- **Two-minute rule during triage.** When reviewing the Milou inbox, anything that takes <2 min gets done immediately instead of becoming a tracked task.
- **Someday/Maybe = parked properties.** Respite, MonoCal, and other parked Tier 1 properties live in the [Investment Tiers](../systems/investment-tiers.md) equivalent of Someday/Maybe.

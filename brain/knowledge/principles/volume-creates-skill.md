# Volume Creates Skill

## In a Nutshell

Do so much volume it would be unreasonable for you to fail. You aren't actually moving toward your goal without doing the reps of the core work required — and you might be avoiding it.

## Source

Alex Hormozi — *$100M Leads* and social media content.

Key quotes:

> "Do so much volume it would be unreasonable for you to fail. Study each success and see what sets it apart from all the failures. Repeat the winning parts even more times."

> "If you suck at cold calls, do 20X more cold calls in order to get better."

His "Rule of 100": do 100 primary actions every day for 100 days. Make 100 cold calls. Spend 100 minutes creating content. Run 100 ads. Pick the core action that moves the needle and do it at a volume that makes failure almost impossible.

## Why It Works

Most people fail because they don't do enough attempts, not because their strategy is wrong. Volume solves two problems at once: it generates results through sheer probability, and it generates skill through repetition. You can't optimize what you haven't done enough of to measure.

The math is simple — if your conversion rate is 2%, you need 50 attempts to get one win. Most people quit at 10 and conclude "it doesn't work."

## How I Apply It

When I catch myself strategizing, planning, or optimizing instead of doing the actual work — that's the signal. The question becomes: what is the core action I'm avoiding, and can I go do 50 of them today?

Examples:
- Growing the newsletter → send 50 personal DMs to potential subscribers
- App Store growth → reach out to 20 reviewers/bloggers this week
- Content marketing → publish 5 posts this week instead of perfecting 1

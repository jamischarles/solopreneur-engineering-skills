# Lion Energy

## A productivity system for neurodivergent solo builders

> Built for one person (me), designed to be shared. This system harnesses ADHD as a superpower instead of fighting it.

---

## The problem

I'm a solo founder with ADHD building VibePulse. I've tried Scrum sprints, OKRs, Gantt charts, and elaborate Notion boards. They all assume the same thing: steady-state output. Plan on Monday, execute Tuesday through Friday, ship on schedule, repeat.

That's not how my brain works.

My brain works like this:

- **I over-plan far out, then throw the plan away.** Six-month roadmaps feel great to create and terrible to follow. By week two, the plan is stale and I resent it.
- **I rotate through roles naturally.** Some days I'm a deep researcher evaluating 15 options in an afternoon. Other days I'm a relentless executor shipping boom-boom-boom. Occasionally I'm a planner zooming out on the roadmap. Rarely, I'm a marketer.
- **I run on lion energy.** Intense sprints of extreme performance — 4 days of focused building where I'm making incredible progress — then the energy fades. Trying to push through the fade makes everything worse. The recovery isn't rest. It's *working on something else*.
- **Novelty is fuel, not a distraction.** Choosing between three tasks toward the same goal keeps me engaged. Being forced into one prescribed task kills motivation.
- **Executive function is the bottleneck.** Not talent, not time, not ideas. The hard part is deciding what to do next when I open my laptop.

Traditional systems fight every one of these patterns. Lion Energy uses them.

## The system at a glance

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  THE BET (the "main novel")                     │
│  One business outcome, 3-14 day appetite        │
│  Shape Up pitch: outcome / appetite / proof     │
│  Energy-driven: sprint until fade, not a timer  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  ROLE MENU (executive function assist)          │
│  2-4 tasks per active role, all toward the bet  │
│  Pick what's interesting. Freedom in focus.     │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  BUCKET GOALS (Hormozi Rule of 100)             │
│  X units marketing · Y units product · Z units  │
│  outreach. Extreme flex in HOW to fill buckets. │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  SANDERSON SWAPS (the "short stories")          │
│  Energy fades → 2-min state capture → swap      │
│  The swap IS the rest. No guilt.                │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  SLOW BURN (background rituals)                 │
│  Daily dogfood · Marketing ammo accumulates     │
│  Weekly build-log · Bug triage as needed        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  MONDAY ZOOM-OUT (10-15 min, Planner persona)   │
│  1. What's the main novel?                      │
│  2. Productive break or avoiding?               │
│  3. Role menu for the week                      │
│  4. Bucket balance check                        │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

The core rule: **you only ever see ONE active bet in front of you.** Everything else is either "up next" (a single fuzzy placeholder) or invisible in the backlog. No six-month roadmap. No 49-issue triage session. One bet, one role menu, one week at a time.

## The bet

Borrowed from [Shape Up](https://basecamp.com/shapeup) by Ryan Singer at Basecamp.

A bet is a business-outcome-framed sprint with an **appetite** — the maximum time you're willing to spend. Not an estimate of how long it will take. An appetite says: "This outcome is worth 7 days of my life. If it takes longer, something is wrong with the shape, not my execution."

Every bet follows a pitch template:

```
Business outcome:  [What changes in the world when this ships]
Appetite:          [3 / 7 / 14 days — hard stop]
Smallest proof:    [The one observable thing that proves it worked]
Out of scope:      [The obvious tempting tangents you'll skip]
Kill criteria:     [What makes you cut losses early]
```

**Fixed time, variable scope.** The appetite is the constraint. If you're running out of time, you cut scope — you don't extend the deadline. This prevents the ADHD trap of "just one more thing" spiraling a 7-day bet into a month.

**The hill chart** is a Shape Up concept for tracking where you are. The left side of the hill is "figuring it out" (unknowns, research, design). The right side is "building it" (known work, execution). If you're past the midpoint of your appetite and still on the left side of the hill, that's the signal to cut scope or kill the bet.

**Ship or kill, never extend.** When the appetite expires, the bet is either shipped (done enough) or killed (not worth continuing). There is no "let me have two more days." If the outcome matters, reshape it into a new bet with a fresh appetite.

## Bucket goals

Borrowed from Alex Hormozi's Rule of 100.

The core insight: building is cheap for me. I can vibe-code all day. Marketing, outreach, sales — those are hard. Without a forcing function, I'll spend 95% of my time building and 5% on everything else. The product gets better but nobody knows it exists.

Bucket goals fix this. Each week (or month), allocate **units of energy** across areas:

```
This week:
  Product:   15 units
  Marketing: 10 units
  Outreach:   5 units
```

A "unit" is deliberately vague — it might be an hour, a task, a pomodoro, whatever feels natural. The point isn't precise measurement. The point is **balance awareness**. During the Monday Zoom-Out, you check: "Am I all-in on building again, or am I actually putting energy into the areas that need it?"

Extreme flexibility in HOW you fill each bucket. If the marketing bucket says 10 units and you feel like writing a Twitter thread, that counts. If you'd rather record a Loom demo, that counts too. The bucket defines the *area*, not the *activity*.

## The four roles

Borrowed from the E-Myth (Michael Gerber) concept that solopreneurs wear multiple hats — but adapted for how ADHD brains actually experience role-switching.

**Researcher.** Goes deep. Evaluates many options quickly before narrowing down. Strength: thoroughness. Risk: rabbit holes that never produce a decision. When active: feed it research tasks from the role menu.

**Executor.** Ships relentlessly. Heads down, focused, boom boom boom. Strength: momentum. Risk: scope creep — gets caught up in momentum and works on things that don't matter. When active: feed it the most concrete build tasks from the menu. The menu is the guardrail.

**Planner.** Zooms out. Attempts to make sense of the roadmap and identify what matters next. Strength: clarity. Risk: over-planning — building elaborate systems instead of shipping. When active: **only on Mondays, only for 15 minutes.** Planner is off-duty the rest of the week.

**Marketer.** Shares the work. Definitely my weakest mode. Has to be in the right mindset, but when I am, I don't mind posting on Twitter or Reddit. Feels a bit narcissistic — "look at me, look at me." When active: **strike immediately.** Marketer energy is rare. When it appears, drop everything and ride the wave. The marketing ammunition is already ready (see below).

Key insight: **you don't choose the role. The role chooses you based on energy.** The system doesn't assign "Tuesday is executor day." It asks: "What role showed up today?" and serves pre-vetted work for that role.

## The Monday Zoom-Out

Borrowed from GTD's weekly review (David Allen) and Bullet Journal's monthly migration (Ryder Carroll).

Every Monday, the **Planner persona** activates for 10-15 minutes. Not longer. This is a zoom-out, not a planning day.

Three questions:

1. **What's the main novel right now?** Is the current bet still the right focus, or should I swap? (The "main novel" metaphor comes from Brandon Sanderson — see Sanderson Swaps below.)
2. **Am I in a productive break or am I avoiding?** If I swapped to a side project last week, honest gut-check: was that a Sanderson Swap (legitimate creative rest) or was I running from a hard problem?
3. **What's the role menu for this week?** For each active role, pick 2-4 tasks from the backlog that serve the current bet's outcome. This is the executive function assist — pre-loading choices so that when Executor or Researcher energy shows up during the week, you don't waste it deciding.

Then: **check bucket balance.** Am I on track for the week's energy allocation across product, marketing, and outreach? Adjust the role menu if needed.

Output: a 1-page role menu. Planner is OFF DUTY until next Monday.

## The role menu

This is the executive function assist — the core innovation for ADHD brains.

The problem: I have 49 open GitHub issues. When I open my laptop with energy to work, the decision of *what to work on* burns through my executive function budget before I write a single line of code. By the time I've triaged and picked a task, I'm drained.

The role menu solves this. Every Monday, the Planner pre-selects 2-4 tasks per active role, all aimed at the current bet's business outcome:

```
Week of April 14 — Bet: "Daily Driver: my dashboard is trustworthy"

🛠 EXECUTOR MENU (pick whichever pulls you)
  1. Fix B006 NaN values on dashboard cards
  2. Fix B007 error state label
  3. Build date range selector UI (#59)

🔬 RESEARCHER MENU (for when you want to dig)
  1. B001 root cause — PostHog query / HogQL / frontend?
  2. #61 split-data key — spike 3 options, 30 min each

📣 MARKETER MENU (only if mood strikes)
  1. Tweet: before/after screenshot of the NaN fix
```

During the week, you open the menu, notice which role has energy, and pick a task. No deciding from scratch. No triage. Just: "I feel like building" → Executor menu → pick one → go.

When rare Marketer energy appears: **strike immediately.** The menu already has marketing tasks ready. Don't let the mood pass while you figure out what to post.

## The Sanderson Swap

Named for Brandon Sanderson, who writes "break novels" between main series. When he gets fatigued on a 400,000-word epic fantasy, he doesn't take a vacation — he writes a 50,000-word sci-fi novella. His "breaks" are bestsellers.

This maps perfectly to how I work. Last week I built three or four new websites. Once I got bored, I swapped back to VibePulse and made incredible progress for four intense days. The websites weren't procrastination — they were the recovery that made the VibePulse sprint possible.

The system:

1. **When energy fades on the main bet**, spend 2 minutes capturing state:
   - What was I doing?
   - Where did I leave off?
   - What's the next step when I return?
2. **Swap to whatever pulls.** A different project, a new website, tooling, process improvement. No guilt. The swap IS the rest.
3. **Monday gut-check:** "Was last week a productive break (Sanderson Swap) or was I avoiding a hard problem?"

The 2-minute state capture is the only discipline the system asks for during a swap. It's the bookmark that lets you resume the main bet without a cold start. Without it, returning costs an hour of "where was I?" context rebuilding. With it, you're back in flow in minutes.

One guardrail for yak-shaving: if you've been improving your process for more than 2 hours straight, pause and ask: "Am I still improving the system, or am I avoiding the work the system is for?"

## Marketing ammunition

The reframe: **"share what you built"** not "look at me."

Marketing feels narcissistic to me. "Look at me, look at me, look at me" is deeply uncomfortable. But "here's what I shipped this week" feels natural — it's showing work, not performing for attention.

The system passively accumulates **ammunition** during sprints:
- Screenshots of before/after
- Shipped items from bet completions
- Milestone moments ("first user onboarded," "100th event ingested")
- Build-log snippets from the weekly slow-burn post
- Demo recordings captured while testing

When the rare marketer mood strikes, the ammunition is ready. You're not staring at a blank tweet wondering what to say. You're picking from a gallery of things you already built and sharing the one that resonates.

No forced marketing days. No marketing sprints. Just: ammo always accumulating, and when the mood appears, the material is there.

## The slow-burn lane

Runs parallel to the active bet, always. Takes 10-30% of time. Never gets its own bet — it's background maintenance.

- **Daily:** dogfood the product (open VibePulse on my own sites)
- **Weekly:** build-log post (1 paragraph + 1 screenshot, any channel)
- **As-needed:** bug triage (B### entries in docs/bugs.md)
- **Monday:** champion review (scan flagged backlog items, promote or archive)

This is Kanban, not Shape Up. Small tasks flowing continuously. The WIP limit is implicit: slow-burn items never compete with the active bet for focus. They're the "water the plants" work.

**Champion labels:** any backlog item can be flagged as a "champion" — something you care about and want to revisit. Champions get surfaced every Monday during the zoom-out. If a champion gets surfaced three times without action, force a decision: promote it to a bet pitch, or archive it. No rotting in the backlog forever.

## Getting started: your first bet

Here's what the first week looks like, using VibePulse's "Daily Driver" bet as the example.

### The pitch

```
Business outcome:  I use VibePulse every morning to check my 3 sites
                   and the numbers feel right
Appetite:          7 days
Smallest proof:    7 consecutive dashboard loads with zero "ugh that's
                   broken" reactions
Out of scope:      Landing page, waitlist, multi-tenant hardening,
                   any new features
Kill criteria:     If B001 perf baseline is still red after 3 days,
                   stop and reshape
```

### Monday zoom-out (sample output)

```
Week of April 14

Main novel: Daily Driver (day 1 of 7)
Productive break or avoiding: N/A — starting fresh
Bucket balance: Product 15 / Marketing 5 / Outreach 0 (low week)

🛠 EXECUTOR: Fix B006 NaN · Fix B007 error label · Build #59 date range
🔬 RESEARCHER: B001 root cause · #61 split-data spike
📣 MARKETER: Tweet before/after of NaN fix (if mood strikes)
```

### Mid-week Sanderson swap (what it looks like)

Wednesday. I've been heads-down fixing B006 and B007. Shipped both. The executor momentum is fading — I keep clicking around the dashboard looking for things to fix instead of starting the date range selector. That's the signal.

**2-minute state capture:**
- What was I doing: Bug fixes on dashboard display
- Where I left off: B006 and B007 shipped. Date range selector (#59) is next.
- Next step when I return: Evaluate 3 date-picker libraries, pick one, build the component.

**Swap:** I go work on a new landing page for a side project. No guilt. Thursday or Friday I'll feel the pull back to VibePulse, open the state capture, and pick up exactly where I left off.
